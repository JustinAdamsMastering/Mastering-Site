FAQ

+
How does the process work?

  1. 1. Send me the final version of your mixed track (or tracks)

    WAV (or AIFF) audio file at the native sample rate (24-bit preferred). Make sure to leave some headroom on the master bus (around -3 to -6 dBFS) to avoid clipping, and turn off any limiting or heavy compression on the master bus.

  2. 2. First pass

    Singles can take up to 3-5 business days, but are often done sooner depending on workload and the needs of the track. EPs run 5-7. Album timelines are worked out per project. The clock starts once all your files are in (and deposit has cleared, if applicable).

  3. 3. Revisions (included)

    Each track includes 3 rounds of revisions. A round consists of one consolidated list of notes from a single designated sender. Additional revisions beyond that are $35 each.

  4. 4. Final delivery

    You receive the final masters and any alternates, cleanly labeled and release-ready. Please credit clearly wherever you can (Mastering Engineer: Justin Adams). Also, when it's out, I'd appreciate it if you sent the link and cover art so I can share it!

+
How do I send you my mix file/files?

Over on the contact page, there is a section where you can paste a shareable link to your mix/mixes for me to download.

+
What audio files do I get back?

Each track you submit is delivered back to you and includes the following three formats: A full-quality WAV master (24-bit), a CD-quality WAV (16-bit/44.1kHz), and a reference MP3. Add-ons such as instrumentals, clean edits, pre-vinyl masters, and DDP images are also available.

+
How does payment work?

Singles are paid in full before delivery of the final masters. EPs and albums require a 50% deposit upfront, with the balance due before finals are released. Full, high-quality masters are shared for review and approval before final payment. Downloadable files will be unlocked once final payment is received.

+
What are DDP images and pre-vinyl masters? Do I need them?

If you're releasing digitally only, you don't need either. If you want a CD or vinyl made of your EP/album, continue reading...

A DDP image file is used by a CD pressing plant to put your EP/album onto disc for official release.

A pre-vinyl master is an alternate version of your main master that's specially designed to be sent to a vinyl pressing plant. Vinyl is pickier than digital, and I make sure your track will hold up on vinyl and not cause problems for the cutting engineer.

I don't press the CDs or vinyl myself but will provide you with the necessary files to take to a CD or vinyl pressing plant.
